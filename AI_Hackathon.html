★ Hackathon Brief

The AI Hackathon

A short, hands-on sprint where teams design, build, and demo a working prototype powered by artificial intelligence.

Duration: 24–48 hours
Team size: 2–5 people
Theme: AI for Good

? What Is It

An AI hackathon is a time-boxed event where small teams turn an idea into a working AI-powered prototype — from blank screen to live demo — in a single weekend.

Participants use modern AI tools (large language models, computer-vision APIs, speech models, agent frameworks) to solve a real problem. The emphasis is on shipping something that runs, not on polished production code.

✦ What It Means

More than a coding contest, the hackathon is a creative pressure-cooker. It means:

  • Learning by doing — you pick up new AI tools faster under a deadline than from any tutorial.
  • Collaboration — designers, developers, and domain experts work shoulder-to-shoulder.
  • Experimentation — bold ideas are rewarded; "boring but safe" is the real risk.
  • Responsibility — every team must consider bias, privacy, and the human impact of their build.

◎ Objectives

By the end of the hackathon, each team should have:

1. Explored a real problem

Identify a concrete pain point where AI adds clear value.

2. Built a working prototype

Ship a demo-able MVP — web app, bot, script, or hardware mock.

3. Applied AI responsibly

Document the model used, data sources, and known limitations.

4. Communicated the idea

Deliver a crisp 3-minute pitch that a non-technical judge understands.

▤ Deliverables

Every team submits the following by the final deadline:

  • Working prototype — a running demo (hosted URL, local app, or recorded walkthrough).
  • Source code — a public or shared Git repository with a README explaining how to run it.
  • 3-minute pitch — live or pre-recorded video covering the problem, solution, and demo.
  • Slide deck — 5–7 slides: problem, users, solution, AI approach, impact, next steps.
  • Ethics note — one short paragraph: what data you used, what the model can get wrong, and who could be harmed.

Demos & judgingEach team pitches; judges score on impact, craft, and creativity.

★ Judging Criteria

  • Impact (30%) — does it solve a real problem for real people?
  • Technical execution (25%) — does the AI actually work?
  • Creativity (20%) — novel use of AI, unexpected angle.
  • Design & usability (15%) — is the demo clear and easy to follow?
  • Responsibility (10%) — honest about limits, data, and risks.
